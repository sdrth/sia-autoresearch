# Adversarial Pass

## Thesis under attack

Activation speed is the core problem. The best bets are to define one activation
metric, eliminate the blank first-login state, and align onboarding to user intent.

## Attack vectors

1. **Survivorship bias in the evidence base.** The sources behind the thesis
   (Lenny, Intercom, First Round) are all written by teams for whom onboarding fixes
   worked. Teams that fixed onboarding and still failed to convert don't write
   playbooks. The thesis could be pattern-matching to published wins.
2. **Activation as a symptom, not a cause.** If trials are coming from the wrong
   ICP, no first-value optimization converts them — the real constraint would be
   acquisition targeting, and onboarding investment would be polishing a funnel fed
   with the wrong people.

## Steelman of the opposing view

The product converts poorly because the wrong users start trials. Activation metrics
will improve, dashboards will look better, and revenue will not move, because users
who reach first value still lack budget, urgency, or authority to buy. The highest
leverage move is upstream: qualify intent before optimizing the first session.

## Counter-evidence queries run

- onboarding improvements no effect trial conversion B2B
- activation metric overrated SaaS criticism
- wrong ICP trial signups conversion problem

## Evidence found

- Counter-search surfaced real cases of onboarding rework that did not move
  conversion — but in nearly all of them the diagnosis was the same: no defined
  first-value event, so teams optimized steps instead of outcomes. That supports
  Bet 1 (define the metric first) rather than undermining it.
- The ICP-mismatch argument is real but testable inside the existing bets: the
  signup-intent survey in Bet 3 doubles as an ICP check. No evidence found that
  ICP mismatch dominates *before* first-value failure for early-stage workflow
  tools with healthy signup volume.

## Outcome

- **Survived intact** (with a sharpened caveat)
- What survived and why: the activation-speed framing held — counter-evidence cases
  reinforced "define the metric before optimizing" instead of refuting it.
- What was revised: memo `## Open questions` now carries the ICP-mismatch caveat as
  an explicit validation item tied to the Bet 3 intent survey; bet order unchanged.
- Score / verdict impact: none upward — `evidenceQuality` stays 0.72 because the
  counter-branch relied on anecdotal cases, not benchmark data. Verdict stays `keep`.
