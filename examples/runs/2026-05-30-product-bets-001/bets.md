# Bets

## Bet 1: Define and instrument one activation metric

**Claim:** Teams without a precise activation event cannot improve trial conversion because they have no measurable target. Defining one verb + object activation event (e.g. "completed first workflow") will make every subsequent onboarding experiment measurable and comparable.

**Build / test next:** Interview 5 recently-churned trial users to identify the action they were trying to take but didn't complete. Use that to define the activation event. Instrument it in your analytics tool. Measure current activation rate as a baseline before changing anything else.

---

## Bet 2: Replace the blank state with a sample project or guided first action

**Claim:** First-login blank dashboards create unnecessary abandonment; users who see templates, sample data, or a guided first action reach value faster. Fixing the empty state is often the highest-ROI onboarding change before broader funnel optimization.

**Build / test next:** Ship a "sample workspace" or template that demonstrates the product's core value with real-looking data. Measure 7-day activation rate for new signups before and after. Target: 10–15 point improvement in activation within 30 days.

---

## Bet 3: Add a one-question intent prompt at signup and fork the first-run flow

**Claim:** Users who are routed to a first-run flow matching their stated intent (e.g. "reduce manual reporting," "onboard new team members," "connect our data stack") complete onboarding at a higher rate than users sent through a generic feature tour.

**Build / test next:** Add a single required question at signup: "What's the main thing you want to accomplish?" with 3–5 job-aligned options. Route each segment to a different first-run checklist (minimum: 3 steps, max: 5 steps). Run for 30 days across new signups. Compare activation rate by segment vs. control (generic flow).
