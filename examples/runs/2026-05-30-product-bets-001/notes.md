## Sources

- [Lenny's Newsletter — What is good free-to-paid conversion](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/what-is-a-good-free-to-paid-conversion) — survey of 1,000+ B2B products: free-trial products convert 8–12% (good) and 15–25% (great); freemium is much lower
- [Lenny's Newsletter — What is a good activation rate](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/what-is-a-good-activation-rate) — defines the activation milestone / aha moment; median SaaS activation ~25–30%; activated users should retain at least ~2x better than non-activated users
- [Lenny's Newsletter — How to determine your activation metric](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/how-to-determine-your-activation) — three-step process for B2B products; includes onboarding guidance from Linear, Figma, Airtable, and Slack growth leaders
- [First Round Review — How Superhuman built an engine to find product market fit](https://review.firstround.com/how-superhuman-built-an-engine-to-find-product-market-fit/) — segment by "very disappointed" users to find who the product is really for before broadening onboarding
- [Intercom — C.A.R.E. framework for user onboarding](https://www.intercom.com/blog/c-a-r-e-simple-framework-user-onboarding/) — progressive disclosure avoids overloading new users; first-login empty states are a common high-friction onboarding pattern
- [ChartMogul — SaaS Benchmarks Report](https://chartmogul.com/reports/saas-benchmarks-report/) — churn is highest in early-stage companies and falls as ICP and onboarding improve; top-quartile monthly customer churn can reach ~1–2%

## Observations

- The #1 pattern in high-converting B2B tools: a single, specific activation milestone defined and tracked — not a generic "account created" event (Lenny activation survey)
- Trial conversion benchmarks vary widely by model; for opt-in free trials, 8–12% is a reasonable "good" band and 15–25% is "great" — well below what teams assume when they only watch signup volume (Lenny free-to-paid survey)
- First-login blank dashboards are a high-stakes empty state: users need guidance, templates, or a clear first action — not a feature dump (Intercom C.A.R.E.)
- Job-to-be-done mismatch: teams often onboard to a feature they built, not the job the user showed up to do — mismatch shows up in early support tickets and intent-misaligned first-run flows (Lenny activation + Intercom onboarding guidance)
- In-app checklists work for simple tools; for complex workflows, progressive disclosure and modular onboarding outperform one-size-fits-all tours (Intercom C.A.R.E.; Lenny cites Linear-style onboarding patterns)
