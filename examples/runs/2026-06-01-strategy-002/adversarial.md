# Adversarial Pass

## Thesis under attack

Verticalize the wedge, not the product: keep the horizontal core, pick one beachhead
vertical from existing user concentration, and ship vertical templates + messaging —
sequenced after the activation metric from run 001 is instrumented.

## Attack vectors

1. **The sequencing is a stall dressed up as strategy.** "Fix activation first, then
   verticalize" could be a way to defer a hard positioning decision indefinitely while
   competitors plant a vertical flag and own the category narrative.
2. **User concentration is a lagging signal.** Choosing the beachhead from where pull
   already exists may just encode who the weak horizontal product accidentally
   attracted — not where the durable wedge is. The "data exercise" could launder a
   biased sample into a strategy.

## Steelman of the opposing view

Verticalize now, fully. Vertical SaaS wins on depth, and the teams that dominate
categories committed early — before their product was clean — and used the vertical
focus itself to force activation clarity. Waiting for a horizontal activation metric
is optimizing a funnel you should be replacing with a vertical one.

## Counter-evidence queries run

- SaaS verticalization too early failure
- vertical SaaS commit early before product-market fit outcomes
- beachhead selection existing customer concentration bias

## Evidence found

- The "commit early and let vertical focus force clarity" cases almost all started
  with a *defined ICP and a working first-value motion* — exactly what run 001 shows
  this product still lacks. The counter-search strengthened the sequencing argument
  rather than the "verticalize now" position.
- The concentration-bias risk is real and could not be dismissed — so it was folded
  into Bet 1 as an explicit check (segment *activated* users, not all signups), not
  waved away.

## Outcome

- **Survived intact** (with one bet sharpened)
- What survived and why: the sequencing thesis held — the strongest "verticalize now"
  case depends on a working ICP/activation motion this product does not yet have, so
  doing activation first is a prerequisite, not a stall.
- What was revised: Bet 1 now specifies segmenting *activated* users to avoid the
  concentration-bias trap surfaced by the attack.
- Score / verdict impact: none upward. `evidenceQuality` stays 0.68 because the
  beachhead choice is still unvalidated until Bet 1 runs; verdict stays `keep`. The
  performed pass is what lets a 0.72 keep clear the adversarial floor (spec §8).
