# Memo

## Thesis

Verticalize the wedge, not the product. Keep the horizontal core, select one beachhead
vertical from existing user concentration (not market attractiveness), and ship
vertical templates and messaging as the test — sequenced after the activation metric
from run 001 is instrumented. Verticalization here is a distribution and onboarding
decision, not a product-architecture decision.

## Context

Run 001 established that activation speed is the binding constraint: no defined
first-value event, a blank first login, and generic onboarding. Meanwhile the broader
market narrative is pushing hard toward vertical SaaS, which creates founder pressure
to "pick an industry" before the core motion works. The question is sequencing, not
ideology.

## Analysis

Three findings drive the recommendation (sources in `notes.md`):

1. **Vertical winners verticalize from strength.** The success cases in the Tidemark
   and a16z material had a working activation motion before going deep. The failure
   pattern is verticalizing to escape a weak core — the weak core comes along.
2. **Templates are a two-for-one.** Vertical template packs show up independently in
   both the verticalization literature (as the cheapest demand test) and the
   onboarding literature (as the standard blank-state fix). Building them attacks the
   run-001 problem and tests vertical pull with the same artifact.
3. **Beachhead selection should follow pull.** First Round-style guidance is
   consistent: pick the vertical where usage, word of mouth, and willingness to pay
   already concentrate. The team has this data and has never segmented it by industry.

## Why this thesis won

The "verticalize now" position died on counter-evidence (sequencing risk on top of
broken activation). The "pure wait" position treated vertical assets and activation
work as competing investments when the evidence says they compose. The winning thesis
is the only one that converts the debate into two cheap falsifiable tests — a
concentration analysis and a template-pack demand test — instead of a bet-the-company
positioning change.

## Implications

- The roadmap from run 001 stands; this run adds a vertical lens to it rather than
  replacing it
- Marketing can test vertical messaging without waiting on product changes
- A full vertical product fork is explicitly deferred, with a stated re-entry
  condition (see open questions)

## Open questions

- Which industries do current activated users actually concentrate in? (Bet 1)
- Does a vertical template pack measurably outperform generic templates on
  first-session activation? (Bet 2)
- What threshold of vertical pull would justify deeper product verticalization —
  e.g. one vertical exceeding 40% of new activated accounts for two consecutive
  quarters?
