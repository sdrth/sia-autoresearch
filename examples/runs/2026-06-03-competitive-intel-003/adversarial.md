# Adversarial Pass

A discard still gets attacked — here the agent attacks its own *null result* by
trying to steelman that real whitespace exists. The attack is what confirms the
discard is honest rather than lazy.

## Thesis under attack

There is no actionable whitespace to occupy from this scan — the category is crowded
and every candidate gap is vendor-asserted, already occupied, or closed. (A null
result, so the attack tries to *prove the opposite*.)

## Attack vectors

1. **Absence of evidence isn't evidence of absence.** Vendor-side sources cannot see
   buyer pain; the real whitespace might be invisible to a landscape scan and obvious
   from one buyer interview. The null result could be a sourcing artifact, not a fact.
2. **A defensible niche may exist below the listicle radar** (e.g. multi-role
   mid-market onboarding) that the broad query simply never surfaced.

## Steelman of the opposing view

The whitespace is real and the scan missed it: complex multi-role B2B onboarding is
genuinely underserved, incumbents are PLG-shaped, and a focused entrant could win it —
the scan failed because it asked vendors, not buyers.

## Counter-evidence queries run

- multi-role B2B onboarding unmet need buyer complaints
- mid-market CS onboarding workarounds community discussion

## Evidence found

- The steelman is *plausible but unsupported by anything in this run* — no buyer-side
  source (community thread, job post, support doc, interview) was reachable within the
  scan's framing. So the attack could not convert the null into a real opportunity; it
  could only show **what evidence the run lacks**.
- That is exactly the discard rationale: the miss is the question framing (vendor-side,
  market-wide) not effort. The steelman becomes the next loop's brief, not this run's
  thesis.

## Outcome

- **Survived as a discard** — the attack reinforced the verdict instead of rescuing it.
- What survived: the null result holds *for this framing*; a buyer-side re-pose is the
  documented follow-up.
- Claims impact: none — a discard forms no durable beliefs, so `claimsDelta` is all
  zero (consistent with the null result; see `verdict.md`).
- Score / verdict impact: stays `discard` at 0.38. The pass does not raise a weak run;
  it just makes the discard defensible.
