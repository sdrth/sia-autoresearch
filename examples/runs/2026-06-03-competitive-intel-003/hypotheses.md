# Hypotheses

## Search strategy

- Starting question: Where is the whitespace in the user-onboarding tooling market that this product could occupy?
- Default search tools used: web search
- Query optimization notes:
  - `user onboarding software market landscape 2026` returned mostly vendor listicles and SEO comparison content
  - narrowing to `onboarding tooling gaps mid-market SaaS` did not materially improve signal — same vendors, same claims
  - the question itself was too broad; "whitespace in a market" without a specific buyer or workflow produced category summaries, not intel

## Candidate hypotheses generated

1. Whitespace exists in onboarding for complex multi-role B2B products
2. Incumbents over-serve PLG startups and under-serve mid-market
3. Pricing models (MAU-based) create an opening for flat-rate positioning
4. AI-generated onboarding flows are an emerging gap
5. The category is consolidating and whitespace claims are mostly noise

## Shortlisted for evidence collection

### Candidate A
- Hypothesis: Incumbents under-serve mid-market
- Queries run:
  - onboarding tooling gaps mid-market SaaS
- Early evidence:
  - only vendor-authored content asserting the gap they happen to fill
- Status: discarded
- Why: no independent evidence; every source had a product to sell

### Candidate B
- Hypothesis: MAU pricing creates a flat-rate opening
- Queries run:
  - product tour tools comparison
  - two vendor pricing pages
- Early evidence:
  - pricing friction is real but already occupied — two vendors position on it
- Status: discarded
- Why: not whitespace if competitors already camp on it

### Candidate C
- Hypothesis: AI-generated onboarding flows are an emerging gap
- Queries run:
  - AI onboarding flow generation tools
- Early evidence:
  - several launches in the last two quarters; crowded, not white
- Status: discarded
- Why: the "gap" closed before this run started

### Candidate D
- Hypothesis: The category is consolidating; whitespace claims are mostly noise
- Queries run:
  - user onboarding software market landscape 2026
- Early evidence:
  - consistent with everything else found, but it is a null result, not a thesis
- Status: discarded
- Why: true-but-useless without a sharper question to act on

## Winning thesis

- Selected hypothesis: none kept
- Why nothing won:
  - the starting question was too broad to produce intel — it produced a category summary
  - source quality never rose above listicles, a G2 grid, and vendor pages
- What a better follow-up loop looks like:
  - narrow to one buyer and workflow, e.g. "how do mid-market CS teams handle onboarding for multi-role accounts today, and what do they duct-tape?" — primary-ish sources (community threads, job posts, support docs) instead of vendor content
